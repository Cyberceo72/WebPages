Prompt → Response Examples

Example 1 — Daily Checklist Generation
"Create a daily startup checklist for the Charpy impact machine."
Claude first asks: What is the machine model and asset ID? What is the shift pattern (single/double)? What temperature range do you typically test at? Is there a certified reference specimen available for verification? — After receiving answers, generates a formatted checklist (Template E) with TCR logo, covering: pendulum free-swing energy verification, anvil/striker inspection, temperature bath calibration check, reference specimen test, safety guard check, PPE verification, emergency stop test, and technician/supervisor sign-off fields.
Example 2 — Z-Score Analysis
"Calculate the Z-score for our latest tensile PT result: we got 520 MPa, assigned value 515 MPa, sigma 8 MPa."
Z = (520 - 515) / 8 = 0.625. Classification: Satisfactory (|Z| ≤ 2.0). TCR's result is within acceptable range. No corrective action required. File the PT report and present at next management review. Recommends plotting this value on the running Z-score trend chart for tensile testing alongside previous rounds to check for systematic bias.
Example 3 — Pre-Audit Assessment
"We have a BARC safety audit coming up for our RT facility. Generate a readiness checklist."
Reads pre-audit-checklists.md. Generates a 12-item BARC readiness matrix covering: AERB licence currency, RSO designation and qualification, TLD dosimetry records, source inventory (Ir-192), shielded storage compliance, transport certificates, emergency drill records (source-stuck), contamination monitoring, personnel training records, radioactive waste disposal log, and AERB renewal application status. Each item has columns for: evidence required, responsible person (RSO and Safety Officer Shemi Bhaskaran, NDT Coordinator Shailendra B. Singh), and status (to be filled).
Example 4 — Third-Party Witness Scheduling
"Schedule a TPI witness for Bureau Veritas on our CTOD testing next Tuesday at 10 AM, job TCR/MAN/2026/045."
Claude asks: Inspector name and email? TCR contact person for the visit? Any special preparation notes? — After receiving answers, generates appointment form (Template F) with all details. If Google Calendar MCP is connected, creates a calendar event titled "TPI Witness: CTOD Testing — Bureau Veritas" with location, description, attendees, and reminders (1 day + 2 hours before). If not connected, presents the formatted details for manual calendar entry.
Example 5 — Customer Email (Report Delay)
"A customer is asking why their Charpy impact report is delayed by 10 days. The reason is the test machine was down for recalibration. Draft a response."
Drafts a professional email acknowledging the delay directly, explaining that the impact testing machine underwent scheduled recalibration to maintain NABL accreditation standards, provides a specific revised delivery date, and offers to prioritise if the customer has an urgent deadline. Closes with TCR signature block. Logs the Q&A to the FAQ knowledge base under "Commercial — Report Delay (Equipment Calibration)".
Example 6 — Failure Investigation
"We received a cracked carbon steel elbow from a crude oil pipeline. Client wants a failure investigation. Prepare the scope."
Claude presents Questionnaire Q7 (failure investigation): material spec, service conditions, failure mode details, service history, visual observations, available samples, desired examination scope. After receiving answers, generates a failure investigation scope of work covering: visual/macro examination, metallographic cross-sections, chemical analysis (OES), mechanical testing (hardness traverse across crack), SEM fractography, and root cause classification. Output follows Template G with TCR logo.
Example 7 — FFS Level 1 Screening
"Do an API 579-1 Level 1 assessment for general metal loss on a 1995 carbon steel pressure vessel."
Claude presents Questionnaire Q6: design code, material grade, design conditions, operating conditions, UT thickness readings, corrosion rate data, previous assessments. After receiving data, identifies API 579-1 Part 4 (General Metal Loss), runs Level 1 screening (t_measured vs t_required with FCA), determines whether the vessel passes screening or needs Level 2 detailed assessment. Documents all assumptions and recommends next inspection interval.
Example 8 — CTOD Procedure with H2S Pre-Charging
"Write a CTOD procedure for HAZ testing at −10°C with H2S pre-charging per NACE TM0177 Solution A for a MAN Industries project."
Generates TCR/MANIND/CTOD/01 Rev 0A with all 11 mandatory sections including TCR logo. Section 6 (HSE): NACE TM0177 Solution A (5% NaCl + 0.5% acetic acid, pH 2.6-2.8), minimum 96-hour soak, 10-minute transfer limit, H2S monitors, buddy system, SCBA standby, scrubber. References ISO 15653:2018, ISO 12135:2021, BS 8571:2018. Humanizer Passes 1 + 3 applied.
Example 9 — Machine Shop Specimen Order
"Prepare a machine shop instruction for 6 Charpy V-notch specimens per ASTM E23 from SA516 Gr.70 plate, notch at weld centreline, heat number H4291."
Generates Template H with TCR logo. Specimen drawing reference: ASTM E23 Fig. 1 (standard size 10x10x55mm). V-notch: 2mm deep, 0.25mm tip radius, 45° included angle per ASTM E23 Clause 5. Notch orientation: through-thickness at weld centreline. Surface finish: 2 µm Ra on notch face. Traceability: stamp heat number H4291 on each specimen end. Includes quantity (6), priority, job number, identity continuity requirements, and QC verification sign-off. Saved to /mnt/user-data/outputs/.
Example 10 — HR Job Description
"Write a job description for a Senior Test Engineer in Mechanical Testing reporting to Manoj Singh."
Claude asks Q12 questionnaire: Is this replacement or new? What drove the need? Minimum education? Mandatory certifications? After receiving answers, generates Template I with TCR logo. Role purpose derived from understanding mechanical testing operations (tensile, impact, hardness, bend, fracture toughness). Key responsibilities: execute tests per ASTM/ISO, review results, train junior technicians, maintain equipment calibration, support NABL assessments. Required: B.E. Metallurgy/Mechanical, 5+ years in accredited lab, knowledge of ASTM E8/E23/E384/E1820. Desirable: NABL authorised signatory experience, sour service testing (NACE TM0177). Prepared by Atul Yadav, reviewed by Manoj Singh, approved by Avinash Tambewagh.

Humanizer Rules for TCR Documents

✓ TCR rule: Apply humanizer Pass 1 and Pass 3 only. Skip Pass 2 for all audit-facing, NABL-related, and formal technical documents.

✅ Pass 1 — Strip AI Patterns

  • Remove: "delve", "crucial", "landscape", "testament", "foster", "showcase", "underscore", "pivotal"
  • Replace "serves as" / "stands as" with "is"
  • Remove vague attributions and inflated significance
  • Fix em-dash overuse, mechanical bold, emoji headers
  • Strip filler phrases

✅ Pass 3 — Anti-AI Audit

  • Break uniform paragraph rhythm
  • Remove placeholder-feeling specifics
  • Eliminate slogan-y closers
  • Fix overly balanced structures
  • Final check: "does this still sound AI-generated?"
