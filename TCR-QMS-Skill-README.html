What This Skill Does

ℹ This skill gives Claude deep TCR Engineering context — 50 years of institutional knowledge, your full NABL scope, every relevant standard across all disciplines, client-specific requirements, and safety rules — so every document it generates is ready for TCR's actual working environment, not a generic lab.

📋 Document Generation

  • Test procedures (all TCR disciplines)
  • Work instructions
  • Method statements
  • NCRs and corrective actions
  • RLA and asset integrity reports
  • Safety manual sections (interim)
  • Gap analyses with RAG status

Prompt → Response Examples

Example 1 — CTOD Procedure with H2S Pre-Charging
"Write a CTOD procedure for HAZ testing at −10°C with H2S pre-charging per NACE TM0177 Solution A for a MAN Industries project."
Generates TCR/MANIND/CTOD/01 Rev 0A with all 11 mandatory sections. Section 6 (HSE) includes: NACE TM0177 Solution A (5% NaCl + 0.5% acetic acid, pH 2.6–2.8), minimum 96-hour soak, 10-minute hard transfer limit, H2S personal monitors, buddy system, SCBA standby, scrubber. References ISO 15653:2018, ISO 12135:2021, BS 8571:2018. Acceptance criteria left blank pending customer input. Revision block included. Humanizer Passes 1 + 3 applied — no AI vocabulary, direct technical language throughout.
Example 2 — NABL Scope Check
"Can we issue a NABL-accredited report for ASTM D638 tensile testing of a polymer composite?"
Reads nabl-scope.md. Confirms ASTM D638 (polymer tensile) is not in TC-6905 scope — it falls under composite/polymer methods which are confirmed out-of-scope. Advises: the test can be performed and reported as a non-accredited test with the standard disclaimer. Revised NABL cert expected Q2 2026 may or may not add this — verify with NABL before quoting under accredited status.
Example 3 — Saudi Aramco Client Spec Interface
"What hardness requirements apply to our carbon steel welds for a sour service Saudi Aramco project?"
Reads client-specs.md and welding-standards.md. Gives: parent metal and weld metal ≤ 250 HV10; HAZ ≤ 250 HV10 (NACE MR0175/ISO 15156 baseline); Aramco SAES-A-301 supplements this with specific alloy additions. Vickers traverse mandatory across parent/HAZ/weld per NACE MR0175 Appendix D. PWHT typically required for CE >0.43%. Notes that TCR cannot approve deviations from SAES on client's behalf — formal written waiver required from Aramco for any delta.
Example 4 — ISO 17025 Corrective Action
"Write a corrective action for a major NCR finding that our tensile test reports are missing uncertainty of measurement."
Produces a formal NCR document (TCR-NCR-[YEAR]-[NN]) with: NC description citing ISO 17025:2017 Clause 7.6 and 7.8.2; root cause (no MU budget established for tensile test, not included in report template); immediate containment (suspend NABL-marked tensile reports until MU established; notify affected clients); corrective action (establish MU budget per GUM, update report template, train authorized signatories); target date; effectiveness verification by internal audit. Severity classified as major — affects accreditation status.
Example 5 — Structural Audit Method Statement
"Write the access and inspection methodology for a structural condition assessment of a 1988 jetty."
Generates methodology section covering: visual inspection of primary/secondary members, cover meter survey, half-cell potential mapping, carbonation depth (phenolphthalein), UT thickness on steel components, photographic documentation protocol. Access specified as scaffolding and MEWP — rope access explicitly excluded as not used at TCR. Condition grading matrix I–IV. Findings structured into P1/P2/P3 priority matrix in the report.

Humanizer Rules for TCR Documents

✓ TCR rule: Apply humanizer Pass 1 and Pass 3 only. Skip Pass 2 (soul injection) for all audit-facing, NABL-related, and formal technical documents at TCR.

✅ Pass 1 — Strip AI Patterns

  • Remove AI vocabulary: "delve", "crucial", "landscape", "testament", "foster", "showcase", "underscore", "pivotal"
  • Replace "serves as" / "stands as" with "is"
  • Remove vague attributions and inflated significance
  • Fix em-dash overuse, mechanical bold, emoji headers
  • Strip filler phrases ("it is important to note that")

⛔ Pass 2 — SKIP for TCR

Pass 2 adds personality, opinions, first-person voice. Not appropriate for:

  • Test procedures and work instructions
  • Safety manuals and HSE documents
  • NABL audit records
  • NCRs and corrective actions
  • Any ISO 17025 compliance document
  • Client submission documents

May apply only to client proposals or marketing copy.

✅ Pass 3 — Anti-AI Audit

  • Break uniform paragraph rhythm
  • Remove placeholder-feeling specifics
  • Eliminate slogan-y closers
  • Fix overly balanced structures
  • Final check: "does this still sound AI-generated?"

Updating: NABL Certificate Renewal

When TCR receives the renewed NABL certificate (expected Q2 2026):

Step 1. Open references/nabl-scope.md.
Step 2. Update Certificate Number, Issue Date, and Valid Until at the top of the file. Change the "Pending" status note to "CURRENT as of [date]".
Step 3. Update the scope tables section by section against the new NABL schedule. Add new disciplines (e.g., full civil IS suite); remove any withdrawn methods.
Step 4. Open SKILL.md. Find the civil scope bullet: "Civil NABL scope: currently partial… revised cert expected Q2 2026…" and update or remove it.
Step 5. Re-upload updated files to the Claude Project (same filenames, overwrite in place).
Step 6. Test with: "What is TCR's NABL scope for civil testing?" — should return the updated scope without the pending-renewal caveat.
⚠ Until the renewed cert is uploaded, Claude will continue to flag the Q2 2026 civil scope caveat on relevant queries. This is intentional — it prevents TCR from inadvertently issuing NABL-marked reports for tests not yet in scope.
🛡

Updating: Safety Manual

When the revised TCR Safety Manual is formally approved and issued:

Step 1. Obtain the final approved Safety Manual PDF. Note the document number and revision.
Step 2. Open references/safety-manual-ref.md. Change STATUS from "UNDER REVISION" to "CURRENT — [Doc No] Rev [X], issued [Date]".
Step 3. Replace the interim requirement content with section references to the actual manual (e.g., "Section 4.3 Working at Height — see Safety Manual Rev 01, p. 22").
Step 4. Open SKILL.md. Find "Safety Manual — STATUS: UNDER REVISION" and update the status line. Remove the note about flagging superseded sections.
Step 5. Re-upload both files to the Claude Project.
Step 6. Test with: "What are the TCR PPE requirements for gamma radiography?" — response should reference the issued Safety Manual section, not the interim gap analysis.

Important Notes

🚫 Rope access is prohibited at TCR. This is a hard rule embedded in the skill. Claude will never include rope access as an access method in any TCR document. If a client spec mentions it, Claude will flag it as outside TCR's operational scope.
⚠ Safety Manual in revision. Safety sections generated by Claude are based on the interim requirements in safety-manual-ref.md (from the Rev 02 gap analysis). All safety outputs must be reviewed by the TCR safety officer before use or site deployment.
⚠ NABL civil scope partial. TC-6905 currently covers aggregates, concrete, and bricks only. Full IS civil suite (cement, soil, bitumen, rebar, tiles) is pending the Q2 2026 revised certificate. Claude will flag this on civil testing queries.
ℹ Acceptance criteria: always verify. Claude quotes acceptance limits from the cited standard. The issuing engineer must verify the limit against the current published edition before signing off any procedure or report.

This skill does not replace:

  • NABL authorised signatory sign-off on accredited test reports
  • Level III review and approval of NDT written practices
  • Safety officer review of HSE documents before site deployment
  • Customer-specific procedure approval where contractually required
  • Engineering judgment on borderline acceptance criteria
  • AERB compliance review for radiography procedures
